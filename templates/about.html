About HeartAI

HeartAI is an advanced machine learning application designed to predict the risk of heart disease based on various health parameters. Our system leverages the power of artificial intelligence to provide quick, accurate assessments that can help individuals understand their cardiovascular health better.

üéØ Project Overview

This project uses a Random Forest Classifier trained on a comprehensive heart disease dataset. The model analyzes 14 different health parameters including age, blood pressure, cholesterol levels, and various cardiac test results to predict the likelihood of heart disease.

üìä Model Performance

  • Algorithm: Random Forest Classifier Ensemble learning method that creates multiple decision trees and combines their predictions for higher accuracy.
  • Training Data Model trained on validated medical data with proper preprocessing and feature engineering.
  • Accuracy Metrics Evaluated using accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score, and ROC-AUC metrics to ensure reliability.

üî¨ Features Analyzed

  • Age: Patient's age in years
  • Sex: Male or Female
  • Chest Pain Type: Different categories of chest pain
  • Blood Pressure: Resting blood pressure
  • Cholesterol: Serum cholesterol level
  • Blood Sugar: Fasting blood sugar levels
  • ECG Results: Resting electrocardiographic results
  • Heart Rate: Maximum heart rate achieved
  • Exercise Angina: Exercise-induced angina
  • ST Depression: ST depression induced by exercise
  • Slope: Slope of peak exercise ST segment
  • Major Vessels: Number of major vessels colored by fluoroscopy
  • Thalassemia: Blood disorder type

üîÑ Data Processing Pipeline

Our system implements a comprehensive ETL (Extract, Transform, Load) pipeline that includes:

  • Data Cleaning: Removing duplicates and handling missing values
  • Feature Engineering: Creating age bins and interaction features
  • Preprocessing: Standardization and encoding of categorical variables
  • Model Training: Random Forest with optimized hyperparameters

üéì How It Works

When you submit your health parameters, the application:

  1. Validates and preprocesses your input data
  2. Applies the same transformations used during model training
  3. Feeds the data through the trained Random Forest model
  4. Generates probability scores for both outcomes
  5. Provides a risk assessment based on the predictions
